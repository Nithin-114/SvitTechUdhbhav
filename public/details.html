Analytics Arena

Rules and Regulations


General Information:

Eligibility:

The event is open to all current students of Engineering Colleges. Participants can register in teams of 3-4 members. Only one team entry per student is allowed.

Event Date & Time:

The event will be held on [Insert Date], and will run for a total of 6 hours. The event will be divided into the following segments:

  1. 30 minutes: Introduction and dataset walkthrough
  2. 3 hours: Data exploration, model building, and analysis
  3. 2 hours: Presentations
  4. 30 minutes: Closing and feedback

Team Composition:

Each team should consist of minimum of 3 and maximum upto 4 members. Teams can be interdisciplinary.

Data Source & Access:

All datasets will be shared with participants at the beginning of the event. Teams will have access to these datasets throughout the competition. Teams are not allowed to use external data sources or pre-trained models unless specified in the problem statement.

Technical Guidelines:


Tool & Platform Usage:

Participants are allowed to use any tool or programming language of their choice (Python, R, etc.) for the analysis and model building.

Recommended platforms include Jupyter Notebooks, Google Colab, or Kaggle Kernels.

All code should be saved in a shared GitHub repository for transparency and tracking.

Collaboration and Resources:


External Resources:

Participants must not use or consult any online resources that directly provide solutions or code snippets (e.g., StackOverflow, GitHub repositories, ChatGPT and similar AI Models, etc.). While it's acceptable to refer to general documentation or tutorials for clarification, any form of direct code copying or pasting from online sources will result in disqualification.

Teams are encouraged to rely on their understanding of data science principles and algorithms, and to solve the problems independently.

Pre-trained Models:

The use of pre-trained models is allowed only if explicitly mentioned in the problem statement. If pre-trained models are allowed, participants must provide proper attribution for their usage and ensure that the models align with the event’s objective.

Code Integrity:

Participants must submit only their original work. Any form of plagiarism, including code, visualizations, or model-building processes taken from online sources, will lead to immediate disqualification.

Data Integrity and Ethics:

Ensure all analysis and modeling is based solely on the provided dataset.

Participants must not manipulate or alter the data in unethical ways (e.g., synthetic data generation without permission).

Ensure all visualizations, code, and analyses are your original work.

Judging Criteria:


Technical Analysis & Model Performance (40%):

Accuracy and effectiveness of the models

Depth of analysis in identifying key factors and relationships within the dataset

Appropriate use of algorithms, metrics, and evaluation techniques

Ability to handle missing or incomplete data

Insights & Presentation Quality (30%):

Clear and actionable insights derived from the data analysis

Quality of the final presentation (clarity, visualization quality, narrative)

Ability to answer questions from the judges with confidence and clarity

Creativity & Innovation (20%):

Uniqueness of the approach to solving the problem

Any additional analysis or innovative solutions (e.g., use of advanced models, deployment)

Adherence to Time & Teamwork (10%):

Completion of the project within the allocated time

Coordination and effective teamwork
